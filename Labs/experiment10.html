Isolation of Active Ingredient in Analgesic Drug

The objective of Experiment 10 is to allow the student to become familiar with some common organic chemistry lab procedures and extract the active ingredient of the analgesic drug assigned to them.

Molecules and Instruments

In this section, students are expected to define the molecular weight, boiling point, melting point, and density of the molecules they will be working with in the lab. Students are also expected to draw the Lewis structure of each of the molecules and include any safety hazards for any of the molecules listed. This allows the student to become familiar with potentially dangerous chemicals and understand the severity of laboratory accidents.

  • Boiling Point: 284 F
  • Melting Point: 139 C
  • Density: 1.4 g/cm3

      Methanol (solvent)
  • Molecular Weight: 32.04 g/mol
  • Boiling Point: 64.7 C
  • Melting Point: 97.8 C
  • Density: 0.79 g/cm3
    		•

  • Mortar and prestle
  • Conical vial (3ml)
  • Pasteur pipette

    • Experiment Outline and Procedures:

    1. Crush asprin/acetominophen sample between 2 sheets of weighted paper
    2. Add the crushed powder to a 3-ml conical vial
    3. With a pipette, transfer approximately 2-ml of methanol solven to vial
    4. Tranfer the conical vial (along with it's content) into the centrifuge tube
    5. Repeat previous step and add 2 additonal ml of methanol solvent to the conical vial
    6. Centrifuge the conical vial for 2-3 minutes
    7. Using a pasteur pipette, prepare the alumina column
    8. Add approximately .5 grams of alumina to pipette
    9. Clamp the pipette and allow the liquid to drain into a new 5-ml conical vial
    10. Evaporate the methanol using a water bath at 50 C
    11. Set up the Hirsh funnel and transfer the material from the 5-ml vial
    12. Use a small sample to determine the melting point
      • By determining melting point of the active ingredient, the student should be able to identify what common analgesic they were given.
